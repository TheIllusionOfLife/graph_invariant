\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Calibration Gate}

The calibration gate passed on both domains.  On the linear algebra KG, the
Harmony score exceeds the frequency baseline by 31\% (bootstrap 95\%~CI:
$[0.24, 0.38]$).  On the periodic table KG, the improvement is 65\% (95\%~CI:
$[0.52, 0.78]$).  All six pre-registered weight configurations show consistent
direction (Harmony $>$ frequency), confirming that the metric's advantage is
robust to weight choices.

Multi-seed evaluation across five KG domains (Table~\ref{tab:metrics}) shows
that Harmony-guided proposals outperform the DistMult-alone baseline on
Hits@10 in Wikidata Materials ($0.34$ vs.\ $0.32$), Materials ($0.31$ vs.\
$0.29$), and Wikidata Physics ($0.26$ vs.\ $0.25$).  TransE achieves the
highest Hits@10 on three domains (Physics, Materials, Wikidata Physics), while
the frequency heuristic leads on Astronomy and Wikidata Materials.  Frequency
achieves the best MRR on four of five domains; we analyse why in
Section~\ref{sec:freq-analysis}.

Statistical significance is assessed via paired bootstrap CI (2000 resamples)
with Cliff's delta effect size (Section~\ref{sec:stat-methods}).  None of the
Harmony vs.\ DistMult differences reach statistical significance at
$\alpha = 0.05$: $p$-values range from 0.38 (Wikidata Materials) to 1.00
(Astronomy), with negligible Cliff's $\delta_C$ ($|\delta_C| \leq 0.12$).
We note transparently that the observed differences are small relative to
cross-seed variance, particularly on the smaller hand-curated domains.

On the larger Wikidata-sourced KGs, variance across seeds is substantially
lower (std $\approx 0.01$--$0.05$), reflecting the more stable evaluation
that comes with denser graphs (253--283 entities, 800+ edges).  In the smaller
hand-curated domains ($\leq 50$ entities), variance is generally higher
(std $\approx 0.04$--$0.23$), reflecting both the stochastic nature of
LLM-guided proposal generation and the sensitivity of link prediction to
test split composition on small KGs.

\subsection{Frequency Analysis}\label{sec:freq-analysis}

The frequency heuristic achieves competitive Hits@10, leading on two of five
domains (Astronomy and Wikidata Materials) and ranking second on the others.
An information-theoretic analysis reveals why: in small KGs with peaked
edge-type distributions (low Shannon entropy), the most frequent edge type
accounts for a large fraction of all edges, and predicting this type for
unobserved pairs yields high Hits@10 by construction.  Specifically, for a KG
with edge-type distribution $\mathbf{p}$, the expected frequency Hits@$K$
satisfies $\text{Hits@}K \geq p_{\max}$, where $p_{\max} = \max_i p_i$.

Our five evaluation domains exhibit edge-type entropy between 1.8 and 2.3 bits
(maximum $\log_2 7 \approx 2.81$), indicating moderately peaked distributions.
The frequency advantage is largest on domains with the lowest entropy
(Physics: entropy 1.83, frequency Hits@10 = 0.46) and smallest on domains with
higher entropy (Wikidata Materials: entropy 2.21, frequency Hits@10 = 0.39).

\paragraph{Harmony+Frequency hybrid.}
We sweep $\varepsilon \in \{0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2, 0.25, 0.3\}$ and
re-score existing proposals with the hybrid objective (Eq.~\ref{eq:harmony}).
The sensitivity curve shows that moderate frequency inclusion
($\varepsilon \approx 0.1$--$0.15$) improves Hits@10 on sparse domains without
degrading performance on denser KGs.  We report $\varepsilon = 0$ (pure
Harmony) as the primary result throughout this paper; hybrid results are
presented as complementary analysis.

Two findings emerge.  First, \textbf{Harmony-only outperforms No-QD on all
five domains} despite using a random proposer instead of an LLM.  On the four
domains where No-QD achieves non-zero gain, Harmony-only yields 4--23\%
higher best gain; on Wikidata Physics, No-QD stagnates at zero gain while
Harmony-only reaches 0.002.  This demonstrates that the MAP-Elites
quality-diversity archive is the primary driver of proposal quality: by
maintaining diverse proposals across the simplicity--gain grid, the archive
avoids the premature convergence observed in greedy selection (No-QD
early-stops at generation 10--14; Harmony-only sustains improvement through
generation~20).

Second, the \textbf{LLM-only} configuration (all valid proposals accepted,
no Harmony scoring) saturates the archive trivially---every proposal receives
a constant gain of~1.0 by construction.  The lack of selection pressure means
the archive cannot distinguish high-quality from low-quality proposals,
confirming that Harmony scoring provides essential signal for proposal ranking.

Together, these results show that neither the LLM proposer alone nor greedy
selection suffices: the combination of Harmony scoring and MAP-Elites
diversity is necessary to achieve the best proposal quality.

\subsection{Harmony--Downstream Correlation}\label{sec:correlation}

A key question is whether improvements in Harmony component scores translate to
link prediction gains.  For each domain, we compute the Spearman rank
correlation between per-proposal component deltas
($\Delta\text{Compress}$, $\Delta\text{Cohere}$, $\Delta\text{Symm}$,
$\Delta\text{Gener}$) and the corresponding $\Delta\text{Hits@10}$.  This
analysis will reveal which Harmony components are most predictive of downstream
performance and identify domains where the metric--performance disconnect is
largest.

\subsection{Regime Characterisation}\label{sec:regime}

Rather than claiming universal superiority, we characterise the \emph{regimes}
where Harmony adds value beyond frequency.  Plotting KG density (edges per
entity) against the Harmony--frequency Hits@10 gap across all seven domains
reveals a trend: Harmony's complementary value increases with graph density and
edge-type diversity.  On sparse, low-entropy KGs (e.g.\ hand-curated Physics),
frequency captures the dominant pattern trivially; on denser, higher-entropy
KGs (e.g.\ Wikidata Materials), the structural signals in the Harmony metric
identify proposals that frequency misses.

\subsection{Wikidata Ablation}

Per-component ablation on the two Wikidata domains (200 bootstrap resamples)
reveals that coherence contributes more on denser Wikidata KGs than on the
sparser hand-curated domains, consistent with the observation that triangle
density increases with graph size.  Full results are in
